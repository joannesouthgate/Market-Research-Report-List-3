<p><p>Fluorine based inert liquids are classified into two main types: PFAS (per- and polyfluoroalkyl substances) and PFPE (perfluoropolyether). PFAS are a group of man-made chemicals used in various industrial applications due to their water and grease resistant properties. PFPE, on the other hand, are synthetic lubricants known for their thermal stability and chemical resistance. Both types of fluorine based inert liquids are widely used in industries such as automotive, aerospace, and electronics for their unique properties and applications.</p></p>
