<p><p>Chromatin Immunoprecipitation (ChIP) testing is a method used to study protein-DNA interactions in cells. There are two main types of ChIP testing based on the type of magnetic beads used for immunoprecipitation: Protein A Magnetic Beads ChIP testing and Protein G Magnetic Beads ChIP testing. These methods involve using specific antibodies attached to magnetic beads to pull down protein-DNA complexes for further analysis. Protein A and Protein G beads have different affinities for antibody classes, allowing for flexibility in experimental design.</p></p>
