<p><p>Semiconductor analog mass flow controllers are typically categorized into two main types: thermal type and pressure type. </p><p>Thermal type mass flow controllers operate by measuring the heat dissipation of a gas stream flowing through a sensor, while pressure type controllers function by monitoring the pressure drop across a restriction in the flow path. </p><p>Both types are widely used in various industries such as semiconductor manufacturing and chemical processing, offering precise control over gas flow rates for critical processes.</p></p>
