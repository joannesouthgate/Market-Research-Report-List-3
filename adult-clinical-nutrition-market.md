<p><p>Adult clinical nutrition market consists of three main types: oral nutrition, enteral nutrition, and parenteral nutrition. Oral nutrition involves consuming food or supplements orally to meet nutritional needs. Enteral nutrition involves delivering nutrients directly into the stomach or intestines through a tube. Parenteral nutrition is administered intravenously, bypassing the digestive system. Each type of nutrition therapy is used based on individual patient needs and ability to consume or absorb nutrients orally. These markets cater to different patient populations with varying nutritional requirements.</p></p>
