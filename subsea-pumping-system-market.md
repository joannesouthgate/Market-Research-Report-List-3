<p><p>Subsea pumping systems are used in offshore oil and gas production to boost fluid flow from subsea wells to production facilities. The Helical Axial Flow Type Pumping System is designed with helical blades to efficiently move fluid in a straight path, while the Twin Screw Type Pumping System utilizes two intermeshing screws to move fluid smoothly. Both systems are essential for maintaining production levels and optimizing the flow of fluids in subsea environments.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Subsea Boosting</li><li>Subsea Separation</li><li>Subsea Injection</li><li>Gas Compression</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Subsea Pumping systems are utilized in various applications within the oil and gas industry including Subsea Boosting, Subsea Separation, Subsea Injection, and Gas Compression. Subsea Boosting systems enhance production rates by providing additional pressure to overcome natural declines in reservoir pressure. Subsea Separation systems separate oil, water, and gas at the seabed, reducing the need for topside processing facilities. Subsea Injection systems inject chemicals or water for enhanced oil recovery. Gas Compression systems compress gas for transportation or re-injection into the reservoir.</p></p>
