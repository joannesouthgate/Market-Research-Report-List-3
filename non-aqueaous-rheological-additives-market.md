<p><p>Non-aqueous rheological additives can be classified into two main types: organic and inorganic rheological additives. Organic rheological additives are derived from natural sources or produced synthetically, offering properties such as improved viscosity control and stability in non-aqueous systems. On the other hand, inorganic rheological additives are typically mineral-based substances that provide benefits such as increased thixotropy and suspension capabilities in non-aqueous formulations. Both types of additives play a crucial role in enhancing the performance and functionality of various non-aqueous products.</p></p>
