<p><p>Neurosurgery operating tables are available in two main types: motorized and non-motorized. Motorized tables offer adjustable height, tilt, and trendelenburg positioning controlled electronically, providing precise movements for surgical procedures. They are often preferred for their convenience and ease of operation. Non-motorized tables, on the other hand, require manual adjustments and are typically less expensive. While they may require more physical effort to operate, non-motorized tables are still widely used in neurosurgery settings for their reliability and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
