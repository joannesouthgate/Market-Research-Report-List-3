<p><p>Hydraulic directional drilling rigs are classified based on the type of ground they are designed to operate in. Soft ground rigs are specifically engineered for drilling in loose or unconsolidated soils, such as clay or sand. They are equipped with features like larger drill bits and higher torque to handle these conditions effectively. On the other hand, hard rock rigs are designed for drilling in more solid and compacted formations, such as granite or limestone. These rigs typically have more robust components and advanced drilling technologies to penetrate hard rock efficiently.</p></p>
